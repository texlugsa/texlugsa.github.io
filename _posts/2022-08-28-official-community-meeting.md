# Meeting Summary

## Location

Online via Skype Video Application

## Summary

The Texas LEGO® User Group - San Antonio (TexLUG-SA) held its eighth virtual Community Meeting of 2022 on 28 August 2022. 15 Group Members participated virtually via Skype from their homes. Attendance virtually is steady and new Members continue to contact us and join Meetings. The Group discussed a variety of topics to include, latest subjects of interest from the LAN or Internet, Members discussed the need at the New Braunfels Railroad Museum for some donated LEGO® Building MOCs, if any Members has some to donate, the greatest number of Members (22) attended Q3 AFOL Shopping Day at the local LEGO® Store at North Star Mall on 10 September 2022, parts have been ordered for the Group's Statue of Liberty Project, again volunteers were sought to display at Krystle's Brick Shop, briefly discussed the pros and cons of displaying in a local mall, a few Members are attending and displaying at Bricks Killeen from 17-18 September 2022 and we briefly discussed the upcoming Fall Train Show in New Braunfels, Texas. An announcement of an in-person Group Social was proposed and several Members are planning to attend the event on 1 October. We briefly discussed in person meetings again, but the consensus is continuing with virtual meetings for now. The Group Members again shared their interests in LEGO® themes they enjoyed. Everyone enjoyed hearing about and seeing Members latest creations and catching up as 2022 continues.

## Attendance

AFOLs 15

## What We Enjoyed Most

Our Virtual Community Meeting is filling the void of seeing each other personally and keeping the Group connected. Virtually allows Members to save gas, a very real thing now and save time by having a Community Meeting online and still accomplish subjects of interest to the Group. Last visit to the LEGO® Store, masks were still being worn by some employees, and a limit of 35 folks were allowed in the local LEGO® Store at one time. Members are encouraging each other to attend and actively participate at the Virtual Community Meeting since we continue to put physical Community Meetings on hold, participation continues to be strong at Virtual Community Meetings. Members continue to show the rest of the Group their LEGO® purchases of new sets and builds of all kinds while working from home or their workplaces physically via these online Skype Virtual Community Meetings.
