# Event Summary

## Location

New Braunfels Convention & Visitors Bureau, 390 Seguin Ave, New Braunfels, TX 78130

[NBRR](https://newbraunfelsrailroadmuseum.org/nbrr/train-shows/)

## Summary

Our Group had 16 Members participate in the second physical Event since the worldwide pandemic hit the USA in March of 2020. We had lots of our folks engaged with show participants more than have seen in a few years at the Fall Show. The folks asked lots of LEGO® based questions plus made comments on some of our more unique LEGO® creations on display. Thanks to all that contributed LEGO® Brick MOCs! The mechanical "Boo Box" was a hit by both young and old! Thanks to all the 8 Wide MOC Building entrants for sharing their creations. Voting was tight and so close that ALL entries will win a prize! The young kids, especially one young lady, loved all the LEGO® Dragons around the display, so she made a point to tell her Grandmother about each of them. Several young boys were counting the superheroes and villains displayed in the LEGO® Marvel Universe MOC, well done! Of course, the LEGO® Harry Potter themed MOC was not to be forgotten as folks looked for their favorite characters. Overall, our theme of Halloween was well represented throughout the display, well done! And last but not least, are the LEGO® Trains that run all around the entire exhibit area of 20 feet x 36 feet plus a special bonus this Fall was the extensive LEGO® Monorail that ran the entire display as well. It provided some exciting moments over the weekend, after we finally discovered that the LEGO® Creator Train and LEGO® Monorail were meeting at a certain point and causing the Monorail to tip over off its track. Switching the direction of the Train solved that potential for future crashes. Several folks commented they would love to have the LEGO® Monorail in their home! Setup for the Event is on Friday afternoon and the Event runs 2 days, 7 hours on Saturday and 6 hours on Sunday. The NBRR Museum planners were extremely pleased with the attendance at the Event with the pandemic still lingering in the State.
