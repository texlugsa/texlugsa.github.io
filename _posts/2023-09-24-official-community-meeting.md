# Meeting Summary

## Location

Online via Skype Video Application

## Summary

The Texas LEGO® User Group - San Antonio (TexLUG-SA) held its eighth virtual Community Meeting of 2023 on 24 September 2023. 13 Group Members participated virtually via Skype from their homes. We discussed a variety of topics: LAN Opportunity - Small Scale Build - Member Robert C's build was accepted and photographed plus model was delivered to local LEGO® Marketing Office, congratulation to Robert!; discussed the collaboration of Members in building a LEGO® Big Texas Comicon Logo for their Convention from 6-8 October 2023 and participation of a maximum of 8 Group Members; Display at Krystle's Brick Shop was extended to 1 October 2023 when Halloween display will be swapped into the front window; a new LEGO® Harry Potter™ display went into the case located at Bricks and Minifigures - Bulverde until mid-December; Robert C has planned a LEGO® designed and built Texas Bluebonnet Workshop for the San Antonio Membership on 21 October 2023 at Krystle's Brick Shop; A mother and son team will be collaborating with Autism Walks! on 7 October 2023 from 0900 - 1300, assisting them with their LEGO® Play Area plus!; future events with the Girl Scouts of Southwest Texas were introduced for the coming months and Brick Rodeo 2024 has opened registration for next year's convention in July in Austin, Texas. During the “Around the LUG” portion of the virtual meeting, LUG Members shared their latest creations and builds.

## Attendance

AFOLs 13

## What We Enjoyed Most

Our monthly virtual Community Meeting continues to achieve one of our goals to keep in touch and plan events and collaboration activities within our Group and with our LUGs in the State for now. Virtually the gathering allows Members to meet from the comfort of their homes and still accomplish subjects of interest to the Group with limited disruption to everyone's busy lives. A good thing is that each Member get a chance to show off builds virtually and discuss the builds challenges or enjoyment of the build. Additionally, each month, the Ambassador relays pertinent information from the LAN and gives Members an opportunity to discuss and comment if they desire. Members are encouraging each other to attend and actively participate at the virtual Community Meeting since we continue to put physical Community Meetings on hold. Virtually allows other potential new Members to drop in and check out the Group and express their interest in joining regularly. Members continue to show the rest of the Group their LEGO® builds via these online Skype virtual Community Meetings.
